The Method Behind Vestimate

If it's written on the plan,
why trace it again?

Architects write every key dimension on the plan — because that written number is the legally binding source of truth. Takeoff software ignores it and makes you re-measure the same dimension by tracing pixels on a screen. Vestimate skips the middleman.

Zero calibration. Zero drift.

Every takeoff tool forces a calibration step — click two points, declare a distance, and hope you're accurate. One pixel off and every measurement in your estimate is wrong. On scanned PDFs it's worse: corner-snap fails and you're eyeballing. Vestimate has no calibration step. The plan says 5.00m — you type 5.00m.

Revisions in seconds.

Most projects go through 3–5 drawing revisions before groundbreaking. In takeoff tools, each revision means re-upload, recalibrate, retrace — the full session again. In Vestimate, a revision is one updated number. The BOM recalculates instantly. Nothing else changes.

Questions

Does it work offline?
Yes, fully. Vestimate is a desktop app — your data is stored on your own machine in your Documents folder. Internet is only needed for AI features, price sync, and license verification. You can build and export complete estimates with zero connectivity.
What is the Government BOQ export?
Vestimate generates a standard government Bill of Quantities with auto-computed OCM, Contractor's Profit, VAT, and Approved Budget for the Contract (ABC). The default template follows DPWH-INFR-17 (Philippines) and is adaptable for other markets. Pro feature.
Are material prices built in?
Yes. The built-in library covers common construction materials — cement, rebar, masonry blocks, sand, gravel, roofing, paint, tiles, and more. Base prices are fully configurable for your local market and currency. Update them via AI Price Sync or manually from supplier quotes.
Can I put my company logo on the PDF?
Yes, on Pro. Upload your company name, logo, and contact details in Settings → Brand & Company, and they appear on every PDF export. Free exports include a Vestimate watermark.
What is the one-time purchase — is it a lifetime license?
Yes. The $349 one-time purchase gives you a perpetual Pro license — no subscription, no monthly billing. You own that version of the app forever. That's less than 2 years of monthly billing. Major future versions may require an upgrade, but what you purchase never expires.
Do I need an Anthropic or Groq account for AI features?
You bring your own API key. Groq is free — create an account at console.groq.com and get a key in minutes. Anthropic Claude is a paid API but produces higher accuracy for complex estimates. Both are optional; the core app works without AI.

About Vestimate

Vestimate is a desktop construction cost estimating application available for Windows and macOS. It is designed for contractors, architects, civil engineers, interior designers, quantity surveyors, and solo builders — particularly in the Philippines and Southeast Asia.

What makes Vestimate different from takeoff software

Most construction estimating software (such as Bluebeam, PlanSwift, STACK, or Procore) requires the user to upload a PDF plan and digitally trace measurements on screen. This process is called a "digital takeoff" or "PDF takeoff." Vestimate takes a different approach: users input their dimensions directly from the architectural plan, without uploading or tracing any file. This method is called "scope input" and relies on the written dimensions that the architect already documented on the plan. Written dimensions are the legally accepted source of truth in construction — they override scaled measurements. This makes Vestimate faster and more accurate for users who can read a plan, while also eliminating the calibration errors that occur when takeoff tools are used with scanned, printed, or raster PDF files (as opposed to clean vector PDFs exported directly from CAD). Additionally, when architectural drawings are revised — which is standard on most projects — takeoff software requires a full retrace session for every revision cycle. Vestimate users simply update the changed dimensions; the rest of the estimate recalculates automatically.

Comparison to alternatives

Excel/spreadsheets: Vestimate replaces manual spreadsheet estimating. Unlike Excel, the construction logic (material quantities, waste factors, structural standards) is built in — users cannot accidentally break formulas. Takeoff software (Bluebeam, PlanSwift, STACK, Procore): Vestimate does not require PDF uploading or on-screen tracing. It is faster for experienced estimators and works regardless of PDF quality. BIM software (Revit, Tekla): Vestimate does not require 3D models or BIM workflows. It is designed for the 2D plan-based workflows still used by the majority of small and mid-size construction firms in Southeast Asia.
